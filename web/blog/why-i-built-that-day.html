Why I Built That Day

I have thousands of photos. You probably do too. They sit on hard drives, backed up and forgotten, organized by folders like "2023 Summer" and "Phone Backup March." Every once in a while I scroll through them and think: what was I doing on this day, three years ago? Five years ago?

The phone will show me "On This Day" — one photo from one year. That's not enough. I want to see July 4th in 2018, 2020, 2022, 2023 — all at once. I want to know who I was with, where we were, what the weather was like. I want a story, not just a thumbnail.

"A photo without context is just a file. Context turns it into a memory."

Not another journal app

There are great journaling apps. Day One is beautiful. But they ask you to write. Every day. Most of us don't. What if the app could do the writing for you — using the data already in your photos?

Your photos contain dates, times, and sometimes GPS coordinates. If you add three words per photo — who you were with, where, and what you were doing — that's enough for an AI to write a warm, personal story about your day. Not fiction. A faithful retelling built entirely on your annotations.

Why desktop, not mobile

Your photo library lives on your computer. Uploading a decade of personal photos to a cloud service is a privacy disaster waiting to happen. That Day runs locally. It scans your drives, reads EXIF data, generates thumbnails — all on your machine. Your photos never leave. Only the annotations and stories sync to the cloud.

What That Day is

That Day is a personal historian. It works like this:

1. Scan your photos — it finds every image across every drive, reads dates, groups them by calendar day across every year.

2. Annotate — three words per photo. Or speak them. The AI fills in the blanks.

3. Seal — your annotations are saved, a story is generated, audio is created, a PDF is made.

4. Wake up — every morning at 8, your story arrives by email. Read it or listen.

What That Day is not

It's not another cloud photo backup. It's not a social network. It's not an AI that invents things you didn't do. Every word in your story comes from your annotations, your dates, your places, and (when GPS is available) your weather. The AI connects the dots. It doesn't make them up.

The name

Every day is that day. The one you forget until something reminds you. A smell. A song. A photo. That Day is that reminder — but daily, systematic, across your entire life.

I built this for myself. I hope it's useful for you too.
